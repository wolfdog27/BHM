Theatre

Dramatic Explorations

Wednesdays 2:30 - 3:30 PM
Grade Range: 3rd - 5th, 5th - 8th, Teen

In this course, students will learn the basics of storytelling and acting through the creation of an original play of their own devising based on the favorite stories of their choosing with a twist! Through this course, students will learn about theatre through analysis of story structure, writing and performing dialogue, stage directions, theatre games, and improvisation. Facilitated discussions and activities will focus on empathy, cooperation, and collaboration through ensemble building driving towards a play written and performed virtually by our intrepid actors.

Movement

Improv

Wednesdays 2:30 - 3:30 PM
Grade Range: 3rd - 5th, 5th - 8th, Teen

In improv class, students will collaborate through scriptless exercises with the objective of connecting thoughts, emotional feeling, and physical movement through a truthful artistic expression of the self. Improv class is an exciting time to let loose, have fun, and be yourself! Class activities will encourage students to speak up and take risks by exploring characters and situations of their own creation. They will problem solve in real time in order to move through out of this world conflicts that arise between these characters, and work their feet with their classmates in order to find resolutions through creative storytelling. Students will be encouraged to practice active listening; through listening we will be able to collaborate to tell stories that are meaningful and fun! Students will leave Improv class equipped with improved life skills such as public speaking, confidence, and flexible thinking that will carry with them through whatever disciplines they choose to pursue.

Matthew Lang

Creative Movement

Mondays 2:30 - 3:30 PM
Grade Range: K - 2nd, 3rd - 5th

In this class we will explore the basics of jazz, ballet, and other contemporary dance techniques and creative movement styles in order to tell stories through musicality and movement in a collaborative fashion. Students will move through these styles and general calisthenics in order to keep their bodies in shape and their minds focused. Each of these explorations will culminate in a fully crafted story through movement pieces for students to perform for the family in the living room, and for grandma on skype!

Matthew Lang
